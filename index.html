THE METHOD

What is the
Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. The idea is devastatingly simple: work in focused sprints, then rest.

01
Pick a task
One thing. No multitasking.
02
Set a timer
25 minutes of pure focus.
03
Work until it rings
No phone. No tab switching.
04
Take a short break
5 minutes. Breathe. Reset.
05
Repeat
Every 4 rounds, take a longer break.
THE SCIENCE

Why your brain
loves it

🧠
Eliminates Decision Fatigue
When the timer is running, the decision is already made. You work. No negotiating with yourself.
⚡
Beats Procrastination
25 minutes feels manageable. Your brain stops resisting and starts running. The hardest part is starting.
🎯
Forces Deep Focus
Constraints create clarity. A ticking clock turns vague intentions into locked-in concentration.
🔋
Preserves Mental Energy
Breaks are not laziness — they are recovery. Your focus muscle needs rest to stay sharp across a full day.
THE RUNODORO TWIST

Focus like a
marathon runner

Sprinting the whole race burns you out. Great marathoners run smart — steady pace, strategic effort, eyes on the finish line.

That is exactly how the best students and workers operate. Consistent, deliberate sessions beat frantic all-nighters every single time.
